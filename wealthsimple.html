The Growth Platform for Wealthsimple's Big-Bet Campaigns

Building repeatable infrastructure that enables bold, culturally resonant campaigns to launch in weeks, not months—while maintaining the trust and simplicity clients expect.

01 — The Challenge

When Speed Becomes Unpredictable

Wealthsimple's growth doesn't come from incrementally optimizing button colors or email subject lines. It comes from bold, culturally resonant campaigns—the home giveaway, the Supreme collaboration, the visceral "Investing for Humans" stories.

Custom Infrastructure Every Time

Every big-bet campaign requires significant custom engineering, operational coordination, and cross-functional heroics.

Learning Compounds Slowly

What worked for one promotion doesn't easily translate to the next. Teams rebuild eligibility logic, recreate communication sequences, and manually track results.

Demand Will Only Intensify

As Wealthsimple scales its product portfolio and audience, the need for these campaigns will grow—but the infrastructure won't keep up without systematic change.

The opportunity: Build a growth platform that makes big-bet campaigns repeatable—enabling the creativity and speed that define Wealthsimple, while maintaining the trust and simplicity clients expect.

02 — Strategic Context

Growth in the AI Era

Traditional growth platforms were built for A/B testing and email automation. The AI era demands something different: platforms that learn, adapt, and compound insights automatically.

Leverage AI for Pattern Recognition

Not just reporting. What worked in the iPhone campaign that could inform the mortgage launch? AI identifies transferable insights across campaigns.

Automate Insight Generation

Teams should spend time on strategy, not spreadsheets. The platform should automatically surface what's working and why.

Enable Rapid Experimentation

AI-assisted copy generation, audience segmentation, and predictive modeling accelerate iteration speed.

Focus on Retention from Day One

Acquisition means nothing if clients don't stick around. The platform must track long-term impact, not just initial conversions.

Efficient growth isn't about doing more—it's about building systems where each action improves the next.

04 — Design Principles

Five Principles for a Big-Bet Growth Platform

The platform needs to embody Wealthsimple's culture and make the right decisions obvious.

1

Default to Clarity Over Cleverness

Every campaign element should be immediately understandable to both clients and the team configuring it. Keep It Simple in practice.

2

Make Trust Non-Negotiable

Clients need to know exactly what they'll get, when they'll get it, and what they need to do. Trust isn't a feature—it's a constraint.

3

Optimize for Learning Velocity, Not Perfection

Every campaign should automatically generate insights that inform the next one. Ship fast, then improve with institutional memory.

4

Enable Creative Freedom Within Guardrails

Marketers shouldn't need engineers to test new messaging or adjust incentives. But they also shouldn't be able to accidentally break compliance or create confusion.

5

Compose, Don't Customize

Instead of building each campaign from scratch, teams assemble them from proven components. Different campaigns might share eligibility logic even if they feel completely different to clients.

05 — Platform Capabilities

Core Platform Capabilities

Four foundational systems that power all campaigns, with AI enhancements woven throughout.

1. Campaign Configuration Engine

Every campaign is fundamentally a set of rules: If a client does X, they qualify for Y, which they'll receive after Z.

The platform enables teams to define:

  • Eligibility criteria that compose like boolean logic—simple individually, powerful in combination
  • Incentive structures with consistent configuration across cash bonuses, product access, physical rewards, and service upgrades
  • Holding periods & milestones with automatic state tracking
  • Capacity & budget controls to prevent runaway costs
AI Enhancement

Natural language configuration ("clients who transferred $5K+ in the last 30 days") that translates to precise logic. AI-suggested audience expansions based on historical campaign performance.

2. Multi-Channel Orchestration

Big campaigns reach clients across email, in-app notifications, push, and physical mail. But orchestration isn't broadcast—it's context-aware communication.

  • Journey mapping that adapts based on client actions and engagement patterns
  • Channel prioritization using historical data to predict highest-impact touchpoints
  • Frequency capping that prevents notification fatigue while ensuring critical updates break through
  • Message personalization with dynamic content and clear fallbacks
AI Enhancement

LLM-powered copy generation that maintains brand voice across channels. Predictive send-time optimization. Automated message variant creation for testing.

3. Trust & Clarity Guardrails

Campaigns can be bold without being confusing. The platform enforces clarity at the configuration stage:

  • Plain-language summaries auto-generated from campaign logic—if it's confusing to the team, it's definitely confusing to clients
  • Progress visibility built into every campaign by default (Am I eligible? What's left? When do I get my reward?)
  • Error recovery paths defined upfront for edge cases
  • Regulatory review triggers for certain incentive types or claim language
AI Enhancement

Automated clarity scoring for campaign terms. AI-generated FAQ predictions based on campaign structure. Proactive identification of potentially confusing edge cases.

4. Measurement & Learning Loops

The platform tracks both short-term performance and long-term impact:

Real-time metrics:

  • Enrollment rate, completion rate, cost per acquisition
  • Channel performance, drop-off points, issue detection
  • Live dashboards that surface problems before they become crises

Post-campaign insights:

  • Cohort retention, lifetime value impact, product cross-adoption
  • Incrementality testing (what worked vs. what was just lucky?)
  • Qualitative signals and surprise discoveries
AI Enhancement

Automated anomaly detection during campaigns. Natural language insights generation ("retention was 23% higher in the 25-34 age group, likely because..."). Predictive modeling for future campaign design.

06 — Decision Framework

Tradeoffs & Decision Filters

Building this platform means making conscious choices about standardization vs. flexibility. Here's how to navigate those tensions.

When Standardization Might Limit Creativity

If the platform only supports three incentive types, what happens when marketing has a brilliant idea for a fourth?

Decision Filter

If a new campaign requires capabilities that 3+ future campaigns could use, build it into the platform. If it's truly one-off, prototype externally but document why.

When Flexibility Creates Risk

If anyone can configure anything, teams will inevitably launch campaigns with unclear terms or broken fulfillment.

Decision Filter

Make common things easy and safe things mandatory. Copy variants don't need approval. Reward fulfillment timing absolutely does.

When Measurement Becomes Performative

If teams track everything, they might optimize for vanity metrics that don't move the business.

Decision Filter

Default to three metrics per campaign: acquisition efficiency, client quality (retention proxy), and learning value (how surprising was the result?).

Build vs. Buy vs. Integrate

Should we build custom or integrate with existing tools like Braze, Iterable, or Segment?

Decision Filter

Build what's strategically differentiating (campaign configuration, learning loops). Integrate where commoditized solutions exist (email delivery, analytics infrastructure).

07 — AI-Native Operations

How Teams Will Work Differently

The platform fundamentally changes how growth teams operate, shifting from manual execution to strategic orchestration.

Campaign Planning

  • Upload past campaign performance → AI identifies successful patterns and suggests audience segments
  • Natural language brief → Platform generates draft campaign structure, eligibility rules, and message flows
  • Competitive analysis → AI summarizes how similar financial services run campaigns and identifies white space

Campaign Execution

  • Platform auto-generates message variants optimized for different audience segments
  • AI monitors performance in real-time and suggests in-flight optimizations
  • Anomaly detection alerts teams to unexpected patterns (positive or negative)

Post-Campaign Learning

  • AI generates structured insights document automatically
  • Pattern recognition across campaigns: "Email-first journeys outperform push-first by 34% for campaigns with holding periods over 30 days"
  • Recommendations for next campaign based on accumulated learnings

The human role shifts: Teams won't disappear—they'll focus on higher-leverage work. AI handles the repeatable, the analyzable, the pattern-matching. Humans handle the bold, the cultural, the judgment calls that define the brand.

08 — Implementation Plan

First 90 Days: From Theory to Reality

If I joined as PM for this platform, here's how I'd start.

Weeks 1-3

Forensic Reconstruction

Map the most successful campaigns as the reference implementation. What systems touched them? What was manual? Where did things almost break? What was learned and immediately forgotten?

Create a journey map showing every decision point, system integration, and client touchpoint.

Weeks 4-6

Design Sprint

Run collaborative sessions with Marketing, Growth, Engineering, and Operations. The goal isn't consensus—it's clarity on the highest-leverage primitives.

Key question: What are the 20% of capabilities that would enable 80% of campaign ideas?

Weeks 7-12

Validate One Workflow End-to-End

Pick the simplest conceptually complete campaign type—likely a referral incentive. Build just enough platform to configure, launch, and measure it without custom code.

Success metrics:

  • Campaign launches in <5 days
  • Post-campaign insights within 48 hours
  • Marketing team can replicate it independently
