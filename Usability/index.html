Usability

User research

Working with users has been a passion for me since I got my first work at 16 where I started to work as a sales representative in a small perfume store. The user research techniques have opened me a world of tools I can use to know deeply the users of the products I’m working on.
User research is one of my favorite things to do every day, I really enjoy talking to the users and empathize about their pain points. I always prefer to make interviews as a qualitative method but I often use polls and A/B tests as quantitative research.

Usability test

This is a powerful tool that we, product managers, have to test if the hypothesis we established to create a solution for our customer's pains are correct or not, so I’m very familiar with this kind of test.
I believe that this kind of test is the best practice to guaranty that the products we develop are really useful for our users, also it has helped me to save money for the company avoiding the work on unnecessary or incomprehensible features (learning fast).

Prototyping

Recently I have discovered an ability to design high and low-fidelity prototypes for user interaction, I don’t have a strong background in design techniques but I continue learning about best practices and tools to prototype software interfaces.
I have been working with Adobe XD, Figma, InVision, Sketch, and recently Illustrator.
