Onboarding Experiments

Growth initiatives to increase Weekly Active Students

The Problem

More than 50% of students who registered on the platform each week abandoned it before having an "aha moment" that would allow them to experience Platzi's value proposition for the first time.

This caused the Weekly Active Students (WAS) metric, which had been proven to have a direct relationship with conversion rate and retention rate, to stagnate and not grow.

Solution Approach

Make adjustments to the onboarding process, where students could select a topic of interest at a general level (faculty) and then something more specific (school), in order to use this information to take them to content that fit what they were looking for.

Experiment 1: Direct to Class

Problem

When I finish the registration and onboarding process, I have to search for the course or subject I need, which takes me a long time to understand the offer that Platzi has for me.

Hypothesis

If we motivate the student to watch a class right after their onboarding, we will increase WAS.

Objective

Modify the current onboarding to encourage students to complete a class right after their registration.

Experiment Design

Type: A/B Test

  • Control group: Current onboarding
  • Experiment group: When they finish onboarding, we take them to the material view to watch a class from a recommended course. Initially, we took all students to watch the first class of the personal branding course.

Learnings

  • While we managed to increase WAS the first week after registration, this effect did not persist beyond that time period. By the second week, students became inactive again.
  • Through interviews and looking at other metrics, we found that students did not watch more than two classes of the recommended course. Some were even confused because they didn't understand why they arrived to watch a class immediately after completing the registration.

Experiment 2: Search-First Onboarding

Problem

When I register on the platform, they ask me about topics I want to learn and suggest some topics. However, the terms used there are not clear to me, so it takes me a long time to find what I was really looking for.

Hypothesis

If we give students the option to specifically search for the content they want, instead of a list of options, we will reduce time to value since they will see content quickly, which will impact WAS.

Objective

Modify the current onboarding to include search functionality, giving students the ability to quickly find the content they want to see and take them to the material view to start watching that content.

Experiment Design

Type: A/B/C Test

  • Control group (C): Current onboarding
  • Experiment group A: Onboarding version that takes them to select a faculty/school and then to material view to watch a class from a recommended course according to the selected school, or the first class of the "Strategies for Online Learning" course when they skip any onboarding steps.
  • Experiment group B: New onboarding version where we add a Google-style window where students can use the search to find the content they want. If they're not clear what to search for, we'll send them to the current steps where they select faculty and school. Once they enter a search criteria, we'll show them the courses returned by the search, and if they select one, we'll send them directly to Material View to watch the first class of that course.

Learnings

  • While this experiment helped more students find initial content they were interested in, it still wasn't a long-term solution where, beyond a couple of classes or a course, students found the motivation to create a study habit.
  • Talking with students, we found that taking them immediately to watch a class from the selected course was not an expected behavior and generated a lot of frustration, as students normally wanted to explore the content a bit more before deciding to start a course.

Experiment 3: Mission-Based Onboarding

Problem

After starting at Platzi, I lose interest because I don't find the motivation to study.

Hypothesis

If we create a longer onboarding process giving students different tasks (missions) they can complete over 4 weeks, we will keep these students active during weeks 2, 3, 4, and 5, so the WAS for these weeks should increase.

Objective

Modify the current onboarding to show in different steps what Platzi's educational offering is, encouraging students to watch a class and complete an entire course.

Experiment Design

Type: A/B Test

  • Control group (Version A): Current onboarding - Students in their 2nd week who haven't gone through any previous experiment
  • Experiment group (Version B): We will show these students 3 missions on the student home with which they can obtain a special badge - Students in their 2nd week who entered group A or B of experiment 2

Learnings

  • Of all the experiments, this was the one that brought us the best results. We effectively got students to generate a study habit after their first month.
  • The missions element turned out to be fun due to its gamification touch, which made new students excited and end up finding the aha moment and then creating a habit around the product during the first month.
  • Talking with students, they confirmed that the missions element was fun due to its gamification touch, which made new students excited and eventually find the aha moment and then create a habit around the product during the first month.

Overall Results

2X
